# Quaeris AI — Homepage Strategy

## The one-sentence bet
Every competitor in this set is selling **speed, self-serve, or 'ask anything.'** Quaeris sells the thing that lets a regulated enterprise actually *ship* agentic analytics: **a number you can defend.** We do not compete on 'fastest answer.' We compete on **'the answer holds up in an audit.'** That is a wedge none of these four own, and three of them (ThoughtSpot, Hex, Wisdom) are circling it without committing.

## Why trust/governance beats speed in THIS market
The whole category has converged on the same hook — 'agentic analytics,' 'ask your data anything.' ThoughtSpot, Wisdom, Lumi, and Hex are now near-indistinguishable in their top-of-page promise. That convergence is our opening. The buyer Quaeris targets — finance, healthcare, insurance data leaders — does NOT have a speed problem. They have a *liability* problem: they cannot put an LLM in front of governed financial data when a hallucinated number could end up in a board deck, a regulatory filing, or a patient decision. Speed is table stakes; **defensibility is the unlock.** So our homepage is not 'look how fast' — it is 'look how every single number traces back to a certified definition, who could see it, and where it came from.' We make governance *visible and beautiful*, not a security badge buried at the bottom.

## The narrative arc (the page tells one story)
The page is a single argument that escalates: **Provocation → Proof → Mechanism → Capability → Foundation → Fit → Validation → Commitment.**
1. **Provocation (Hero):** 'Your AI gives confident answers. Can it prove they're right?' — then immediately a *live* ask→governed-answer moment that resolves the tension on screen.
2. **Proof of stakes (Trust bar):** the cost of a wrong number, named industries, the honest compliance posture.
3. **Mechanism (How governed answers work):** the 3-step trace — question → certified metric → governed source — so the skeptic understands the *architecture*, not just the vibe.
4. **The differentiator (Semantic layer + lineage):** the governed semantic layer as our named, branded IP — the thing nobody can answer a question without.
5. **Capability (interactive tabs):** show the surfaces — intelligence brief, dashboards, agents — all powered by the one governed engine.
6. **Foundation (Warehouse-native + security):** data never moves, role-gated at query time, audit trail, honest cert status.
7. **Fit (Solutions by role + industry):** finance/healthcare/insurance self-select.
8. **Validation (Social proof):** honest, design-forward proof without fabricating logos or metrics.
9. **Commitment (Closing CTA):** dual path — see a governed answer (self-serve-ish) and talk to us (enterprise).

This order converts because it answers the regulated buyer's questions *in the order they actually ask them*: 'Can I trust it?' → 'Why should I believe you?' → 'How does it actually work?' → 'What's the magic?' → 'What can it do?' → 'Is my data safe and where does it live?' → 'Is it for me?' → 'Who else trusts this?' → 'How do I start?' Every competitor here either buries the mechanism (Wisdom names it but never shows it) or shows capability before earning trust (Lumi, ThoughtSpot). We earn trust *first*, then spend it.

## Borrow-vs-avoid, per competitor

### ThoughtSpot — *the enterprise-serious incumbent*
**BORROW:**
- **Named-agent family** as an organizing device, but reframed around governance roles (not 'SpotterViz/Code'). Our agents should be named for what they *guarantee*, not what they render.
- **Analyst-authority-high-on-page** instinct — except we don't have a Gartner badge yet, so we substitute *governance proof* high on the page instead of borrowed authority.
- **Persona-lane navigation** — let finance vs. data-team vs. compliance self-select.
**AVOID:**
- **Product-light hero.** Their #1 weakness — concept-heavy hero, no product visual, '15-second-rule failure.' We fix this by putting a *live governed-answer moment* in the hero itself.
- **Generic enterprise-dark mood** indistinguishable from Snowflake/Databricks/Palantir. Our purple/lavender + Roboto/Inter is a deliberate anti-dark-mode identity. We will be the one screenshot in the category that is instantly recognizable.
- **Decorative, metrics-light logo wall.** We won't fabricate logos; our proof is governance artifacts, not borrowed brands.

### WisdomAI — *our closest philosophical competitor (they say 'accuracy is the hard part')*
**BORROW:**
- **The teaching framework.** Their 'four types of context' is the smartest device in the set — it reframes the problem before selling the solution. We build our own: **'The four things a number needs to be trusted'** (Definition, Permission, Lineage, Audit) — and ours maps 1:1 to features we actually have.
- **Name the IP.** They brand the 'Adaptive Context Engine' as a recurring anchor. We brand our **Governed Semantic Layer / 'Trust Engine'** the same way — a memorable, repeated proper noun.
- **'Meets your data where it lives'** displacement line → our **warehouse-native, zero-copy, zero-egress** message, which is sharper because it's literal.
- **Contrarian sharp hook** ('Accuracy is the hard part') → ours is **'Confidence is easy. Proof is hard.'**
**AVOID:**
- **Zero product visuals** — their single biggest failure. We are the opposite: governance made *visible* on screen everywhere.
- **Single demo-only CTA** with no mid-funnel path. We offer a self-serve 'see a governed answer' moment.
- **Anonymous, hollow social proof** and a self-asserting 'we're the top platform' headline with no evidence. We never assert leadership we can't show; we *demonstrate* trust live.
- **Closing CTA that's a copy of the hero** — no arc. Our close escalates.

### Lumi AI — *the ROI/outcome-first operator*
**BORROW:**
- **Embed a real working conversational demo** (chat → SQL → chart), not a video. This is their strongest move — *show, don't tell.* Ours becomes the **'ask → governed answer with citation'** moment, which is strictly better because it adds the trust layer they lack.
- **Aggregate value counter** ('$150M+ insights identified') as a design pattern — but we won't fabricate a number. We use an *animated stat counter* on something we can honestly substantiate (e.g., 'metric definitions governed,' 'queries audited,' or a clearly-labeled illustrative figure) rather than inventing customer ROI.
- **Three-tier-but-disciplined CTA** thinking — we'll do TWO, not three (they admit three caused paralysis).
- **Direct competitive contrast line** ('reveal opportunities dashboards miss') → ours: **'Other AI tools answer fast. We answer in a way you can defend.'**
**AVOID:**
- **Two competing hero headlines** fighting each other — we commit to ONE anchor.
- **Narrow vertical framing** (supply-chain-first) that alienates other buyers. We lead horizontal (governed analytics for regulated data) then branch by industry.
- **Auto-rotating testimonial carousel** (UX antipattern) — static quote cards.
- **Sitemap-dump footer** — our footer ends on a CTA moment.
- **Fabricated-feeling specificity** like 'seven-figure opportunity in 30 days' that we can't back. Our boldness is about *proof*, not promised ROI.

### Hex — *the design/PLG benchmark, also playing the trust card*
**BORROW:**
- **The single-dataset narrative thread.** Their best idea: one fictional company (NexaCorp) runs through every section, making the page feel like one continuous workflow. We adopt this hard — **one illustrative governed dataset (a finance scenario) threads the entire page**, so the hero question, the lineage, the audit trail, and the dashboards are all *the same story*.
- **Agent observability as a visible product section** — showing the admin/audit view as proof of governance without text claims. This is *directly* our territory; we make the **audit-trail timeline** a hero-class visual.
- **Role-specific, outcome-specific testimonial format** (title + company + concrete workflow) — when we have real quotes.
- **Light-mode, premium, Linear/Vercel-density aesthetic** over enterprise-dark — validates our purple/lavender clean-premium direction.
- **Lead with the headline proof point** (they surface $100M before the hero).
**AVOID:**
- **Trust message that stays abstract** — Hex says 'trust' but never plainly explains the *mechanism* for a non-technical exec; their semantic-model section is a cold YAML view. We explain the mechanism in plain language AND show it beautifully — the governance is the *star*, not a YAML afterthought.
- **Forgettable fictional data** ('NexaCorp,' galactic product names). Our illustrative dataset is a *believable finance scenario* a CFO recognizes.
- **Notebook-first framing** that reads 'for data teams' despite 'anyone' messaging. We resolve the tension: business user asks, governance guarantees, data team trusts — one page, no contradiction.
- **Hidden pricing / PLG evasiveness FUD.** We keep Pricing in nav and don't feel evasive.

## What makes us unmistakably different
1. **Governance is the show, not the footer.** Everyone else puts SOC-2 badges at the bottom. We make lineage, role-gating, and audit trails the *centerpiece visuals*, top-of-page.
2. **A live ask→proof moment in the hero** — we beat the product-light heroes (ThoughtSpot, Wisdom) on the 15-second rule AND we add the citation/trace layer Lumi and Hex's demos lack.
3. **A named framework + named engine** (the four trust requirements + the Trust Engine) — borrowed discipline from Wisdom, executed with features we actually ship.
4. **One finance dataset threaded through the page** (Hex's trick) but *believable and governed*, so the whole page is one auditable story.
5. **Radical honesty as a trust signal** — we state SOC 2 Type II *in progress*, HIPAA *on roadmap*, GDPR *supported*. In a category full of hollow self-assertion (Wisdom) and decorative proof (ThoughtSpot), candor IS differentiation for a compliance buyer.
6. **A distinctive visual identity** — purple/lavender, Roboto+Inter, light-premium — the anti-enterprise-dark. The one homepage in the set you'd recognize from a thumbnail.

## Section blueprint

###  Sticky Navigation (`nav`)
- **Headline:** Quaeris
- **Interaction:** Mega-menu dropdowns: Platform lane (Trust Engine, Semantic Layer, Agents, Audit & Lineage, Warehouse-Native, Security). Solutions lane splits by ROLE (Finance, Data/Analytics teams, Compliance/Risk) and INDUSTRY (Finance, Healthcare, Insurance). Scroll-aware: nav condenses and the filled CTA gains a subtle purple glow after the hero passes.
- **Mock-dashboard:** no

###  Hero — The live governed-answer moment (`hero`)
- **Headline:** Confidence is easy. Proof is hard.
- **Sub:** Quaeris is the agentic analytics platform for regulated data teams. Business users ask in plain language — AI agents return governed, audited answers where every number traces to a certified definition. Zero hallucinations, by design.
- **Interaction:** An animated 'Intelligence Brief' answer card. The user sees a plain-language question typed into a query bar, a brief 'agents working' state (semantic-layer lookup → role check → query), then the answer card assembles: the number, a green 'Certified metric' chip, a one-line 'traces to: net_revenue (Finance-certified, owner: FP&A)' citation, and a 'Show the trace' affordance that expands into the lineage mini-view. Optional 2-3 suggested follow-up questions are clickable to re-run the animation. This is our answer to Lumi's chat demo and Hex's inline thread — plus the trust layer they lack.
- **Mock-dashboard:** yes

###  Stakes & Honest Posture Bar (`trust-bar`)
- **Headline:** Built for the data you can't afford to get wrong.
- **Interaction:** Animated stat counters that tick up on scroll-into-view. A subtle horizontal 'governance guarantees' marquee (Certified · Role-gated · Audited · Warehouse-native) — static feel, not a rotating-quote antipattern.
- **Mock-dashboard:** no

###  How a governed answer works (the mechanism) (`how-it-works`)
- **Headline:** From question to proof in three governed steps.
- **Sub:** Other AI tools answer fast. We answer in a way you can defend.
- **Interaction:** A 3-step horizontal stepper that auto-advances on scroll (or click-to-advance). As each step activates, the relevant part of the intelligence-brief card lights up: the query bar (Ask), a role-badge + semantic-match overlay (Govern), the citation + audit chip (Prove). Connecting lines animate left-to-right in brand purple.
- **Mock-dashboard:** yes

###  The Differentiator — Governed Semantic Layer + Lineage (our named IP) (`differentiator-semantic`)
- **Headline:** No number leaves Quaeris without a definition behind it.
- **Sub:** Meet the Trust Engine — our governed semantic layer. Every metric is certified once, owned by a person, and reused everywhere. The AI can't invent a number, because it can only answer with definitions you've approved.
- **Interaction:** An interactive lineage / semantic-layer visual: the certified metric 'net_revenue' sits at the center; lines fan out to (a) its source tables in the warehouse, (b) the dashboards/agents that consume it, and (c) its owner and certification date. Hovering any of the four framework pills (Definition/Permission/Lineage/Audit) highlights the corresponding part of the graph. Click the metric to flip to its definition card (the actual SQL/business logic, owner, last-certified).
- **Mock-dashboard:** yes

###  Capabilities — One governed engine, every surface (`capabilities-tabs`)
- **Headline:** One Trust Engine. Every way your team works.
- **Interaction:** A tabbed capability dashboard (our existing component). Tabs swap the central product mock with an animated transition. Each tab's mock uses the SAME finance dataset, so switching tabs feels like moving through one workflow, not four disconnected features. Stat counters or 'certified' chips animate on tab activation.
- **Mock-dashboard:** yes

###  Warehouse-Native + Security — The Foundation (`warehouse-native-security`)
- **Headline:** Your data never leaves your warehouse. We bring the agents to it.
- **Sub:** Quaeris queries Snowflake, BigQuery, Databricks, Redshift, and Synapse in place. No copy. No egress. Role-gated at query time. Every question logged.
- **Interaction:** An audit-trail timeline visual: a vertical, time-stamped log of a single question's lifecycle — 'User asked → role verified (Analyst, APAC scope) → matched certified metric net_revenue → queried Snowflake in-place (no data copied) → answer returned → entry written to audit log.' Each event expands on click to show detail. A small toggle shows a 'denied' example (a user without permission asking the same question) to prove role-gating visibly.
- **Mock-dashboard:** yes

###  Solutions — by Role & Industry (`solutions-by-fit`)
- **Headline:** Governed analytics, tuned to how you're regulated.
- **Interaction:** A segmented control (Role / Industry) that swaps a set of three outcome cards. Each card has a one-line pain → one-line governed outcome, and a 'See it for [persona]' link that deep-links into a tailored sub-page. Cards are static (no auto-rotation).
- **Mock-dashboard:** no

###  Social Proof — Honest & Design-Forward (`social-proof`)
- **Headline:** Proof, not promises.
- **Interaction:** Our comparison-table component: rows = the four trust requirements + warehouse-native; columns = 'Quaeris' vs. 'Black-box AI BI.' Checkmarks animate in on scroll. Quote cards (when available) are static with a subtle hover lift.
- **Mock-dashboard:** no

###  Closing CTA — The Commitment (escalated, not repeated) (`closing-cta`)
- **Headline:** Put a number in front of your auditor. Watch it hold.
- **Sub:** See exactly how a plain-language question becomes a governed, traceable answer — then bring it to your hardest data.
- **Interaction:** A condensed re-trigger of the hero intelligence-brief animation with a fresh question, ending on the 'certified + audited' state — visually closing the loop the hero opened. The two CTAs sit directly beneath it.
- **Mock-dashboard:** yes
