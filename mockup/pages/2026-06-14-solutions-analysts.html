Questions analysts ask

Straight answers. No hype.

Can I ask follow-up questions, or just one-shot queries?

Yes, agents remember context across messages. Ask your first question, then refine, drill down, or pivot. Agents chain analyses without losing state — so you can start with churn, ask a follow-up about a specific segment, and then forecast forward, all in one session.

What if the semantic layer doesn't have the metric I need?

Agents tell you directly: "Metric X doesn't exist in the certified layer." Then your data team decides whether this is a new metric to add, or whether you should ask differently. You're never stuck — you get transparency instead of a hallucinated number.

How long does setup take?

Semantic layer migration typically takes 1–2 weeks for teams with 50–200 metrics. After that, analysts can start asking immediately. No data import, no model rebuilding — your existing warehouse is the source. Quaeris connects to Snowflake, BigQuery, Databricks, Azure Synapse, and Redshift.

What happens if I misinterpret an answer?

That's why every answer shows its sources. Click a metric name and you see the definition, business rules, and data lineage. Misinterpretation is caught because you see exactly what was queried — there's no mystery layer between you and the certified data.

Is this a replacement for our BI tool?

No — it's a complement. Quaeris handles exploratory questions and ad-hoc analysis. Your dashboards and reporting tools stay in place for structured, scheduled reporting. Different tools for different jobs. The same certified metrics power both.

Can I share answers with colleagues?

Yes. Copy the answer, the metric definitions, and the audit trail. You can export to Slack, email, dashboards, or notebooks. Every export is reproducible — colleagues can re-run your exact analysis and get the same answer from the same certified source.

What if a metric definition is wrong?

Your data team fixes the definition in the semantic layer once. Every agent answer going forward uses the corrected definition — across all analysts, all tools, all queries. No individual spreadsheet chasing. No rework across 40 dashboards.

How much training do analysts need?

Minimal. If you can ask a colleague a question, you can ask Quaeris. Onboarding is typically 1–2 hours. After that, most analysts are running their own analyses without support. Technical analysts who understand SQL often find they're faster with Quaeris — they spend less time writing queries and more time interpreting answers.
